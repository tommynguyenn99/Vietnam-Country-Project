Discover the delicious flavors of Vietnam with these 3 must-try dishes

Pho

Phở is a traditional Vietnamese soup dish consisting of broth, noodles, herbs, and meat, typically beef or chicken. It is considered the national dish of Vietnam and is popular in households, street stalls, and restaurants across the country.-Wikipedia

Banh Mi

Bánh mì is a type of Vietnamese short baguette with a thin, crisp crust and soft, airy texture. It is commonly filled with savory ingredients, such as meats and vegetables, and condiments like pâté, mayonnaise, and chili.-Wikipedia

Bun Bo Hue

Bún bò Huế is a popular Vietnamese rice noodle dish that consists of sliced beef, chả lụa, and sometimes pork knuckles. It originated in the city of Huế, which is known for its royal court cooking style.-Wikipedia

Explore your taste buds with these 3 exciting dishes!

Banh Xeo

Bánh xèo is a popular savory fried rice pancake in Vietnam known for its crispy texture and sizzling sound. It is made with a mixture of rice flour, water, and turmeric powder and commonly stuffed with ingredients such as pork, shrimp, green onions, mung beans, and bean sprouts. -Wikipedia

Com Tam

Cơm Tấm is a classic Vietnamese dish made of cooked rice with broken rice grains, referred to as tấm. Cơm Tấm is often served with traditional accompaniments like grilled pork ribs, thin strands of seasoned pork, Vietnamese-style steamed omelet with meatloaf or an omelet/fried egg, chopped scallions lightly fried in oil, sliced cucumber, tomato, and pickled vegetables, and mixed fish sauce. -Wikipedia

Banh Bot Loc

Bánh Bột Lọc is a Vietnamese dish of clear, chewy tapioca dumplings, filled with shrimp and pork belly and topped with fried shallots. Served as an appetizer or snack, it originates from the city of Huế.-Wikipedia
